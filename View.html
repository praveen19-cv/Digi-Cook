Hyderbad Chicken Biryani

Ingredients:

For Marinating the Chicken:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For Assembling the Biryani:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden brown
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • Fried cashews and raisins for garnishing (optional)

Procedure:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:

    In a bowl, mix together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt.

    Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

  2. Cooking the Rice:

    In a large pot, bring water to a boil.

    Add soaked and drained rice, green cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaf, and salt.

    Cook the rice until it's about 70-80% cooked. The grains should still have a bite to them. Drain the rice and set aside.

  3. Assembling the Biryani:

    In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the marinated chicken at the bottom.

    Spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken.

    Sprinkle half of the fried onions, mint leaves, and coriander leaves.

    Drizzle half of the saffron milk and ghee over the layers.

    Repeat the layers with the remaining chicken, rice, fried onions, mint leaves, coriander leaves, saffron milk, and ghee.

  4. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

    Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can also use dough to seal the edges of the pot to trap the steam.

    Place the pot on very low heat (sim) for about 20-25 minutes. This slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together.

  5. Serving:

    Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers.

    Garnish with fried cashews and raisins, if desired.

    Serve hot with raita or salan (a spicy gravy) on the side.
