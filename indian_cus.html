Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ajwain seeds (optional)
  • Water, as needed

  • For the filling:
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, salt, and ajwain seeds (if using).
  2. Add oil and mix until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add water and knead to form a firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, ginger, and green chili. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add peas and diced potatoes. Mix well.
  6. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
  7. Remove the filling from heat and let it cool.
  8. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval shape.
  9. Cut the oval in half to form two semi-circles.
  10. Take one semi-circle, wet the straight edge with water, and form a cone shape. Seal the edges.
  11. Fill the cone with the potato filling. Wet the open edges and pinch to seal, ensuring the samosa is tightly closed.
  12. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  13. Carefully slide the samosas into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  14. Remove from oil and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  15. Your samosas are ready to be served! Enjoy with chutney or sauce.

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (sliced onions, spinach leaves, potato, cauliflower, etc.)

  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Steps:

  1. Mix the sliced vegetables in a bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine gram flour, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, and salt.
  3. Add this dry mixture to the mixed vegetables and mix well.
  4. Sprinkle water little by little and mix until the vegetables are coated with the batter. The batter should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  6. Once the oil is hot, drop spoonfuls of the batter-coated vegetables into the oil.
  7. Fry the pakoras until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove them using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or sauce of your choice.

Dahi Puri Recipe

  • Prepare the Puri:
    1. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
    2. Gently place small, round puris in the hot oil.
    3. Fry until they puff up and turn golden brown.
    4. Remove and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Prepare the Filling:
    1. Boil and peel potatoes. Cut them into small cubes.
    2. Mix the potatoes with finely chopped onions and boiled chickpeas.
    3. Add salt, chaat masala, and a pinch of red chili powder. Mix well.
  • Assemble the Dahi Puri:
    1. Gently make a hole in the center of each puri.
    2. Fill the puris with the prepared potato mixture.
    3. Add a spoonful of sweet tamarind chutney and mint chutney to each puri.
    4. Top with a dollop of thick yogurt.
    5. Sprinkle with more chaat masala and red chili powder.
  • Garnish and Serve:
    1. Garnish with finely chopped coriander leaves and sev (crispy chickpea flour noodles).
    2. Serve immediately to enjoy the crunchy, tangy, and spicy flavors.

    Ingredients:

    • 1 cup rice
    • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
    • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water for soaking and grinding
    • Oil or ghee for cooking

    Steps:

    1. Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together. Soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
    2. After soaking, drain the water and grind the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together to form a smooth batter. Add water gradually as needed while grinding. The batter should be slightly thick. Add salt to the batter and mix well.
    3. Allow the batter to ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours. The batter will rise and become slightly airy.
    4. Once the batter is fermented, mix it gently to get a uniform consistency.
    5. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
    6. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the pan. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
    7. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and cook until the edges start to turn golden brown and crispy.
    8. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook the other side for a minute or until it's cooked and slightly crispy.
    9. Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or any desired accompaniment.

    Ingredients:

    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • Water for kneading
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil or ghee for cooking
    • Optional fillings (e.g., potato, paneer, spinach, etc.)

    Steps:

    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt.
    2. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft and smooth dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
    3. If using any fillings, prepare them by mixing the ingredients together and seasoning as desired.

    13. Flip the paratha and cook the other side. Apply oil or ghee on both sides and cook until golden brown and crispy.
    14. Remove the paratha from the skillet and place it on a plate.
    15. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
    16. Serve the hot and delicious parathas with yogurt, pickle, or any desired accompaniment.

    Ingredients:

    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil for frying
    • Water for kneading

    Steps:

    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it's smooth and pliable. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
    2. In a pan, heat a tablespoon of oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    3. Add the mashed potatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for a few minutes to combine the flavors. Remove from heat and let the potato mixture cool down.
    4. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls. Take a dough ball and flatten it slightly. Place a spoonful of the potato mixture in the center of the dough.
    5. Gather the edges of the dough to enclose the potato filling and pinch to seal. Flatten the stuffed dough ball gently.
    6. Roll out the stuffed dough ball into a round poori using a rolling pin. Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls.
    7. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in a poori and gently press it with a slotted spoon. The poori will puff up. Fry until both sides are golden brown.
    8. Remove the fried poori from the oil and place it on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
    9. Serve the hot and puffy aloo pooris with your favorite side dish, such as potato curry, chana masala, or pickle.

    Ingredients:

    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 1 lb (450g) chicken, mutton, or vegetables (your choice)
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
    • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
    • 2 teaspoons biryani masala powder
    • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
    • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon saffron strands (soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk)
    • Whole spices: 2-3 bay leaves, 4-6 cloves, 4-6 green cardamom pods, 1-2 cinnamon sticks
    • Salt to taste
    • Water for cooking rice

    Steps:

    1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
    2. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil or ghee. Add the whole spices (bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) and sauté until fragrant.
    3. Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
    4. Add the ginger paste and garlic paste. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    5. Add the chopped tomatoes, biryani masala powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and the oil starts to separate.
    6. If using meat, add it now and cook until it's browned on all sides. If using vegetables, you can skip this step.
    7. Stir in the yogurt and marinate the meat or vegetables for about 30 minutes.
    8. In a separate large pot, bring water to a boil and add the soaked rice. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
    9. Layer the marinated meat or vegetables with the partially cooked rice in the pot. Sprinkle saffron milk over the rice.
    10. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and place it over low heat. Cook for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
    11. Once done, remove the pot from heat and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before gently fluffing the biryani with a fork.
    12. Serve the biryani hot with raita, salan, or your favorite side dish.

    Ingredients:

    • 1 liter full-fat milk
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 4 cups water
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
    • Chopped nuts for garnishing (e.g., pistachios, almonds)

    Steps:

    1. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan, stirring constantly to prevent sticking.
    2. Add lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring until the milk curdles. This will separate the curds and whey.
    3. Place a muslin cloth or cheesecloth over a sieve and strain the curdled milk. Rinse the curds under cold water to remove any sourness from the lemon juice or vinegar.
    4. Gather the cloth and squeeze out excess water. Hang the cloth for about 30 minutes to drain any remaining whey.
    5. Knead the drained chenna (curdled milk solids) on a clean surface for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft.
    6. Divide the chenna into small portions and roll them into smooth balls without any cracks. Make sure there are no air bubbles inside the balls.
    7. In a separate pot, combine sugar and water and bring it to a boil to make a sugar syrup.
    8. Add the chenna balls to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover and cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes. The balls will puff up as they cook.
    9. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands to the syrup for flavor.
    10. Remove from heat and let the rasgullas cool down in the syrup.
    11. Once cooled, refrigerate the rasgullas for a few hours before serving.
    12. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.

    Ingredients:

    • 2 cups of khoya (milk solids)
    • 1 cup of sugar
    • 1/4 cup of ghee (clarified butter)
    • 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder
    • A pinch of saffron strands
    • Chopped nuts for garnishing (e.g., almonds, pistachios)

    Steps:

    1. Heat the ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan on low heat.
    2. Add the khoya and sauté it for a few minutes until it softens.
    3. Add the sugar and mix well until the mixture starts to leave the sides of the pan.
    4. Add the cardamom powder and saffron strands, and mix again.
    5. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly.
    6. Grease a tray or plate with ghee.
    7. Transfer the mixture to the greased tray and spread it evenly, about 1/2 to 3/4 inch thick.
    8. Sprinkle the chopped nuts on top and gently press them into the mixture.
    9. Allow the barfi to cool and set for a few hours.
    10. Once set, cut it into desired shapes, and your barfi is ready to be served.
